Probability Theory

Measure-theoretic probability and its foundations.

Key Texts

  • Billingsley, P. Probability and Measure (3rd ed.) — the standard graduate text
  • Durrett, R. Probability: Theory and Examples — excellent for limit theorems
  • Kallenberg, O. Foundations of Modern Probability — comprehensive and rigorous

Foundations

Kolmogorov’s axiomatization: a probability space (Ω, ℱ, P) consists of a sample space, a σ-algebra of events, and a probability measure. The σ-algebra requirement is what forces us into measure theory — we need to be careful about which sets are “measurable.”

Limit Theorems

The law of large numbers and central limit theorem are the workhorses of applied probability. Less appreciated: the rate of convergence, the Berry-Esseen theorem, and situations where the CLT fails (heavy tails, dependence).

Random Processes

  • Martingales: the right framework for sequences with “no exploitable structure”
  • Markov chains: memoryless processes, spectral gaps, mixing times
  • Brownian motion: the continuous limit of random walks, foundational for stochastic calculus
